Id3ntity V3ctor Engin3 (IVE_333)
Canonical Document — v1.0 (Locked)

Scope and Intent
IVE_333 is a descriptive conceptual engine for understanding identity as a relational, enacted, and temporally sustained phenomenon. It does not provide self-help guidance, moral instruction, or prescriptive advice. It describes how identity becomes intelligible, interactive, and durable within shared social reality.

Core Premise
Identity is neither purely internal nor purely external. Identity only exists if it is first identified, enacted, and affirmed by the self. It is then reinforced and co-authored by the world when it is recognized, reaffirmed, and sustained over time. Identity is relational and processual, not innate, fixed, or granted.

Structural Operators (Analytic, Not Moral)
Experience — invested labor structure: identity is enacted through effort, repetition, practice, and lived experience.
Money — external recognition structure: money functions as a proxy for external legibility within systems of exchange, signaling visibility or institutional acknowledgment without defining worth.
Time — longevity and continuity structure: identity stabilizes, erodes, or transforms through persistence across time.

Recognition vs Validation
Recognition and validation are not the same. Recognition is ontological, not emotional: legibility, acknowledgement of presence, and admission into shared reality. Validation is evaluative: approval, reward, praise, or moral agreement. Recognition answers whether an identity is intelligible and addressable; validation answers whether it is rewarded or endorsed. Recognition is a precondition for interaction; validation may or may not follow.

Clarification: Not Code-Switching
This framework is not code-switching. Code-switching is a strategic alteration of expression to navigate power asymmetries. Recognition here names the structural condition under which an identity is legible at all. Code-switching is a strategy; recognition is a condition. This model does not prescribe adaptation to dominant norms.

Signal Environments
Contemporary identity formation occurs within high-velocity signal environments (platforms, forums, algorithmic systems). These environments function as recognition fields that accelerate feedback and compress time. They do not confer truth or worth.

Descriptive, Not Prescriptive
IVE_333 does not instruct individuals on how to behave. It maps how identity becomes intelligible, how recognition differs from validation, how labor, visibility, and time interact, and how identities persist or dissolve. No moral claims are made.

Final Assertion
Identity emerges through self-identification, enacted experience, recognition within shared reality, and temporal endurance. IVE_333 maps this process without judgment.
